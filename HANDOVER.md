## PROJECT SUMMARY

"Das erste Wort" is a 100-chapter German language course built as standalone HTML files. It teaches German through etymology, historical narrative, and pattern recognition — NOT through traditional memorization. The core insight: English speakers already "know" ~80% of German vocabulary through shared Germanic roots and Latin/Greek borrowings. The course teaches the DECODER RING (sound shifts, prefixes, suffixes, compounds, grammar logic) that makes those words visible.

The user is **Toby** (admin@g2gadvisory.co.uk), founder of G2G Advisory. The primary tester is **Veronica** (a Mandarin Chinese speaker — hence "Chinese bridges" connecting German/English/Chinese in etymology cards).

## GOLD STANDARD: CHAPTER 7

**Chapter 7 (`chapter7.html`, 98KB) is the gold standard for what every chapter should look like.** It has ALL features working correctly. Use it as the reference when fixing or writing other chapters. Key elements:

### Visual/Canvas
- Unique thematic canvas animation (sparks rising from a forge — orange/red, NOT generic starfield)
- Dark background with era-appropriate gradient
- Canvas sits behind content with z-index layering

### HTML Structure
```
<body>
  <!-- G2G branding (top right, fades on hover) -->
  <div style="position:fixed;top:0;right:0;z-index:200;...">
    <a href="https://www.g2gadvisory.co.uk">
      <svg ...>G2G logo</svg>
    </a>
  </div>

  <!-- Scroll progress bar -->
  <div id="progressBar"></div>

  <!-- Canvas background -->
  <canvas id="particleCanvas"></canvas>

  <!-- Main content wrapper -->
  <div class="content">
    <!-- Chapter header -->
    <!-- Narrative scenes with etymology cards -->
    <!-- Interactive guess sections -->
    <!-- Quiz section (gated) -->
    <!-- PATTERNS DISCOVERED (this chapter's new patterns) -->
    <!-- YOUR PROGRESS (fat clickable bars with expandable tables) -->
    <!-- WORD COLLECTION (animated cards for revision) -->
    <!-- Chapter end + navigation -->
  </div>

  <!-- G2G footer -->
  <div style="...">A G2G Advisory Project · ...</div>

  <script>
    // Canvas animation
    // Scroll effects
    // speakGerman() audio function
    // Etymology card expand/collapse (classList.toggle)
    // Quiz logic
  </script>
</body>
```

### Interactive Features
1. **Etymology cards**: Gold German words are clickable. Clicking expands a hidden card with etymology, IPA, Chinese bridge, English cognate. Cards use `classList.toggle('open')` with `max-height` CSS transition.
2. **Audio pronunciation**: Each card has a 🔊 speak button. Uses Web Speech API: `speechSynthesis`, `lang: 'de-DE'`, `rate: 0.85`. The `speakGerman(word)` function is defined in the `<script>` block.
3. **Inline German audio**: ALL German words in narrative text (even those without etymology cards) should be clickable for audio pronunciation via `onclick="speakGerman('...')"`.
4. **Interactive guess sections**: "What do you think X means?" with clickable option buttons and immediate feedback.
5. **Quiz**: End-of-chapter quiz. 80% pass threshold to unlock word collection. Questions get progressively harder across chapters (early chapters: 6 questions; later: 8-10+). Cumulative review questions test older material.
6. **Progress bars**: Two fat (14px) animated bars:
   - **Words Collected** (gold gradient): X / 850 (Y%)
   - **Patterns & Grammar** (purple gradient): X / 145 (Y%)
   - Both are CLICKABLE — expand to show a full table of all words/patterns collected so far, with Word, Meaning, and Chapter columns.
7. **Word collection**: Animated cards at the bottom showing this chapter's new words with staggered reveal.
8. **Navigation**: Prev/next chapter buttons at the bottom.

### End-of-Chapter Order (IMPORTANT)
The correct order at the end of each chapter is:
1. Patterns Discovered (this chapter's new patterns — in an aside-box)
2. Your Progress (fat clickable progress bars with expandable detail tables)
3. Words Gathered (animated word collection cards)
4. Chapter End text
5. Navigation buttons (prev/next)

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Voice & Tone
- Intimate, first-person narrative — like an essayist talking directly to ONE reader
- "This is your first German word. And you never had to learn it."
- NOT a textbook. NOT a fantasy game. NOT a classroom.
- The writing voice evolves: more hand-holding in Phase 1, more trusting in later phases
- Chinese bridges (connecting German/English/Mandarin) should feel organic, not systematic

### Visual Design
- Dark theme: `#0a0e1a` background, `#d4cabb` text, `#e8a44a` gold for German words
- Font: Cormorant Garamond (loaded via Google Fonts)
- Max-width: 560px centered, responsive with 768px and 600px breakpoints
- Each chapter has a UNIQUE canvas animation thematically tied to its story (NOT generic starfields)
- G2G Advisory branding: subtle purple SVG logo top-right, footer at bottom

### Pedagogical Approach
- **Pattern-based, not memorization-based**. The course teaches the decoder ring.
- Sound shift rules unlock thousands of words at once
- Prefix/suffix/compound patterns are generative — they let you decode NEW words
- Grammar is taught as logic ("why does German do this?") not as rules to memorize
- Quizzes force active retrieval — reading alone doesn't build lasting memory
- Progress bars motivate by showing cumulative achievement
